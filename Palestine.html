A Living History

Palestine through
the centuries

Ancient — 1917
A Land of Ancient Civilisation
Palestine has been continuously inhabited for thousands of years — a crossroads of civilisations, home to Canaanites, Philistines, Hebrews, Romans, Byzantines, Arabs, and Ottomans. The land's culture, art, and architecture reflects millennia of rich, layered history.
1948
Al-Nakba — The Catastrophe
The establishment of the State of Israel resulted in the forced expulsion of over 700,000 Palestinians from their homes. This event, known as the Nakba, created a refugee crisis whose repercussions continue to define the Palestinian experience to this day.
1967
Occupation of Gaza & West Bank
Following the Six-Day War, Israel occupied the West Bank, East Jerusalem, and the Gaza Strip. This marked the beginning of one of the longest military occupations in modern history, reshaping Palestinian daily life under a system of checkpoints, blockades, and restricted movement.
2007 — Present
The Gaza Blockade
Since 2007, Israel has imposed a land, sea, and air blockade on Gaza, severely restricting the movement of people and goods. The resulting humanitarian crisis has left 2.3 million people dependent on international aid in what UN officials have described as an open-air prison.
October 2023 — Present
The Current War
Following the Hamas attacks of October 7th, Israel launched an intensive military campaign on Gaza. The scale of destruction has been described as unprecedented — flattening entire districts, displacing nearly all of Gaza's population, and triggering a full-scale famine.

Gaza is one of the most densely populated territories on Earth — 2.3 million people living in an area of just 365 square kilometres. Before the current war, it was home to universities, hospitals, markets, orchards, and centuries of culture.

"Gaza is not just a place. It is a wound in the world's conscience that refuses to close."

Each city and refugee camp within Gaza has its own distinct identity, its own history of resilience, its own relationship to the sea, the land, and the olive groves that once stretched across this coastal strip. These are not statistics — they are communities.
